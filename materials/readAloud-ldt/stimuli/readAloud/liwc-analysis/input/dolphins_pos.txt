Dolphins are intelligent sea creatures who mostly prefer warmer, tropical waters.  A thick layer of blubber helps with buoyancy and serves to streamline their flexible bodies.  They have excellent hearing, which is adapted for both air and water.  Sociable and playful, dolphins live in pods of up to a dozen individuals.  In Greek mythology, dolphins were esteemed as the helpers of humankind.  Seeing dolphins swimming in the wake of a ship was considered a good omen by the Ancient Greeks and many ancient coins pictured a young man riding on the back of a dolphin.
