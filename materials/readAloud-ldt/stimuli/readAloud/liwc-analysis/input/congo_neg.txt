Both industrial logging and slash-and-burn agriculture, where sections of trees are cut down and then burned to cultivate crops, have led to major deforestation in the region. Deforestation has made the area more accessible to commercial hunters, igniting a poaching epidemic that is killing off midsize animals faster than they can reproduce. And social unrest means that the dangers of ambush, kidnapping, and robbery remain high in some areas.
